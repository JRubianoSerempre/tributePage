Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

The last universal genius

Here's a time line of Leibniz's life:

  • 1646 - Born in Leipzig, Sajonia (Germany),son of Friedrich Leibniz, professor of moral philosophy at the University of Lepizig.
  • 1653-1661 - Early education at the Nicolai School in Leipzig.
  • 1661-1663 - Studies under Jacob Thomasius at the University of Leipzig; bachelor's thesis on scholastic theories of individuation (1663); attends lectures of Erhard Weigel at the University of Jena (summer 1663).
  • 1663-1666 - Legal studies in Leipzig; receives doctorate of law from University of Altdorf (1666); publication of On the Art of Combinations.
  • 1667-1671 - Resides in Nuremburg, Mainz, and Frankfurt; secretary to Johann Christian von Boineburg, minister to the elector of Mainz.
  • 1671 - Letters to Hobbes, Arnauld; publication of the New Physical Hypothesis.
  • 1672-1676 - Travels to Paris on diplomatic mission for the elector of Mainz; begins mathematical studies under Christian Huygens; first trip to England (1673), where he is elected to the Royal Society on the basis of his model of a four-function calculating machine.
  • 1675 - Discovery of the differential and integral calculus.
  • 1684 - On a New Method for Maxima and Minima, first published account of the calculus.
  • 1687-1690 - Journey through Austria and Italy to research the history of the House of Brunswick; formulation of the science of dynamics
  • 1695 - Publication of Specimen dynamicum and New System of the Nature and Communication of Substances, and of the Union of the Soul and the Body.
  • 1700 - Founding of the Berlin Academy of Sciences, with Leibniz as its first president.
  • 1716 - dies at the age of 70.
